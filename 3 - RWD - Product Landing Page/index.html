Different colours

The color of apple peel occurs in shades of green, and after maturing also yellow or red, often mixed. The pulp takes on a very light - near-white or pale yellow color, while the layers lying close to the skin may be slightly green, pink, sometimes with visible "veins" - depending on its color. The apples may be peeled, and then the bruise becomes soft and brown.

Juicy flesh

The taste of ripe apple apples is sweet and sour. The apple flesh is stiff, crunchy and full of transparent juice. After long storage the apple becomes soft and wrinkled, but still edible. After slicing or peeling with access to oxygen, the apple pulp begins to oxidize on the surface, which turns brown.
